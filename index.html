GOVERNMENT REAFFIRMS COMMITMENT TO DIGITAL EDUCATION


On 28 March, 2026, Information and Media Permanent Secretary Thabo Kawana, represented by ZANIS Director Loyce Saili, officiated at the launch of the KEMZ Academic Centre School Website and Internet Café in Kanyama-Lusaka.


Here are the highlights:


• The launch of the KEMZ Academic Centre School Website and Internet Café underscores Government’s commitment to promoting access to information, innovation and technology driven learning as key pillars of national development.


• Reaffirmed that digital transformation is central to Government’s development agenda, emphasising that the future belongs to societies that invest in digital literacy, innovation and access to information.


• Emphasised that digital inclusion must begin in schools, as learners must not only consume information but also create it, evaluate it critically and use it responsibly in a rapidly evolving digital environment.


• Noted that KEMZ website and internet café represent more than just infrastructure, as they provide opportunity, access and empowerment to learners, parents and the wider community through enhanced connectivity and digital services.


Through this initiative:


i. Learners will be able to conduct research and access credible academic resources.


ii. Students will participate in online learning and extend education beyond the classroom.


iii. Parents will engage more effectively with the school through digital platforms.


iv. The community will benefit from reliable internet access through the internet café.


• The initiative directly supports the 2025 Competence Based Curriculum, which emphasises practical skills and real-life application of knowledge, with ICT competence positioned as a foundational requirement in modern education.


• Stressed that while technology is a powerful tool for education, innovation and connectivity, it must be used responsibly, as lack of discipline and guidance can lead to misuse and misinformation.


• Urged school management to continue investing in digital infrastructure, teacher training, cybersecurity and digital safety measures to ensure that technology enhances education rather than distracts from it.


• Learners were encouraged to take advantage of technology for innovation, problem solving and community development, with a call to master technology rather than be controlled by it.


• Parents and guardians were urged to play an active role in guiding learners to navigate the digital space responsibly, with discipline and purpose.


• Called on other institutions across the country to emulate such initiatives, emphasizing that collaboration between schools, communities and Government is key to accelerating national development and achieving a digitally inclusive Zambia.


• Meanwhile, KEMZ Academic Centre School Director Kennedy Muyango said the initiative aligns with Government’s digital transformation agenda and the 2025 Competence Based Curriculum by equipping learners with practical ICT skills for a modern, knowledge driven economy.


• He added that the launch also supports Government’s goal of digital inclusion by expanding access to technology, online learning and information for learners, parents and the wider community.


— Report by Ministry of information and media
